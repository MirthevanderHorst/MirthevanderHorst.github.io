Pocket Knife

What do you do with the smallest pieces of scrap wood? Of course you would not throw them away! This pocket knife was a bit of a fun experimental project. The pieces of Walnut were a bit too small to use for anything directly, so they had to be joined together to be wide enough for the knife. This project started around 2019 when I had no tools at all, so everything had to be done with a file and rusty handsaw. The fact that it worked out so well really surprised me! The blade is cut out from an old flat connector plate that is locked either closed or opened, using the mechanism of a pen hidden inside the handle. Though the metal is too soft to properly sharpen and use for anything other than butter, it was a very fun experiment!

Materials used

  • Solid Wenge
  • Solid Walnut
  • Scrap Metal
  • Pen mechanism for the blade lock
  • Leather (lining the inside to protect the blade)
  • Osmo Oil
